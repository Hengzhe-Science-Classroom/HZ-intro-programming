# Introduction to Programming · Interactive

**Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs**

**Status:** Planned

Part of [Hengzhe's Science Classroom](https://hengzhe-science-classroom.github.io/hub/) · Tier 3 · Undergraduate

## What You'll Learn

- Expressions, variables, and functions
- Recursion and iteration
- Data abstraction
- Higher-order functions
- Object-oriented programming
- Interpreters and metalinguistic abstraction

## References

- Harold Abelson & Gerald Jay Sussman, *Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs* (2nd ed., MIT Press)
- Brian Harvey & Matthew Wright, *Simply Scheme* (MIT Press)
- Al Sweigart, *Automate the Boring Stuff with Python* (2nd ed.)

## Features (when built)

- **Interactive visualizations** — every concept comes alive with draggable, animated demos
- **KaTeX math rendering** — rigorous notation throughout
- **Exercises with hints & solutions** — from computational to proof-based
- **Progress tracking** — saved locally in your browser
- **Zero dependencies** — just open the link and learn
